Architecture, myth, knowledge, and grandeur — four places that defined human ambition across the ages.

Spain

LA SAGRADA FAMÍLIA

Barcelona, Spain

Antoni Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece — a soaring basilica of stone and light that has been under construction since 1882, blending Gothic and Art Nouveau into something entirely its own.

Architecture UNESCO
Greece

COLOSSUS OF RHODES

Rhodes, Greece

A bronze statue of the sun god Helios that once stood 33 metres tall at the entrance of Rhodes harbour — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, destroyed by earthquake in 226 BC.

Ancient 7 Wonders
Iraq

HANGING GARDENS

Babylon, Iraq

Said to have been built by King Nebuchadnezzar II for his wife, who longed for the green hills of her homeland — a cascading garden in the desert, the only Wonder whose existence remains unconfirmed.

Mythical 7 Wonders
Egypt

LIBRARY OF ALEXANDRIA

Alexandria, Egypt

Once the greatest repository of knowledge in the ancient world — holding scrolls from every civilization. Its destruction remains one of history's most lamented losses of human learning.
